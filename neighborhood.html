Westfield, NJ is a picturesque suburban town located about 16 miles soutwest of New York City but at the same time close enough to enjoy the prisitine beaches of the Jersey Shore on a regualar basis. The orgins of the town date all the way back to 1699 when it took it's name from the rich "West Fields of Elizabeth Town" and is home to many historical colonial landmarks.

The town's current population is 31,032 and ranks as the 99th highest-income place in the U.S with a median income of $159,923 in 2018. It was also ranked as the 30th safest city to live in the U.S back in 2014. Westfield is well known for its famous downtown area, which has one multiple national awards. Featuring over 40 restaraunts/casual dining establishments, 200 retail establishments, and 400 commercial enterprises, the area is a regional destination to visit in New Jersey.

Throughout the year several festivals take place such as Spring Fling and Festifall and throughout the summer every Tuesday night is jazz night where live performances occur outdoors. Finally, the town has a memorial plaza near the downtown area with memorials dedicated to WWII, the Korean War, the Vietnam War, the Spanish - American War and September 11th, which pays special tribute to the residents of Westfield who died in the September 11 terrorist attacks.
